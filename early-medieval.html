About The Early Medieval Company

The Early Medieval Company represents the period from 800-1100AD, a time of significant change in England, from the Viking Age through the Norman Conquest. Our group focuses on the rich cultural heritage of this era, including Viking influences, early medieval trade, and the development of medieval society.


What We Do

  • Historical re-enactment of early medieval life
  • Demonstrations of period crafts and skills
  • Participation in medieval markets and events
  • Educational displays and workshops

Join Our Group

Whether you're interested in combat, crafts, or historical research, we welcome new members who share our passion for the early medieval period. No experience is necessary - we provide training and guidance for all aspects of medieval re-enactment.

Timeline of Significant Events (800-1100)

793

The Viking raid on Lindisfarne marks the beginning of the Viking Age in England.

871

Accession of Alfred the Great, who begins the unification of England and establishes important cultural and educational reforms.

878

The Treaty of Wedmore establishes the Danelaw, dividing England between Anglo-Saxon and Viking rule.

899

Death of Alfred the Great and accession of Edward the Elder, who continues the unification of England.

937

The Battle of Brunanburh establishes Æthelstan as the first true King of all England.

978

Accession of Æthelred the Unready, whose reign sees renewed Viking attacks and the payment of Danegeld.

1016

Cnut the Great becomes King of England, establishing a North Sea Empire.

1042

Accession of Edward the Confessor, the last Anglo-Saxon king of England.

1066

The Battle of Hastings and the Norman Conquest of England by William the Conqueror.

1086

Compilation of the Domesday Book, a comprehensive survey of England's land and resources.

1087

Death of William the Conqueror and accession of William II.

1095

The First Crusade is called by Pope Urban II, marking the beginning of the Crusades.
